Why this works & further suggestions

Dark rail, bright workspace

A flat deep navy (derived from brand blue #1c6dd0 — a single solid colour, not a gradient) frames the work area so the heatmap and practice screens feel brighter and more focused. The white active pill is impossible to miss.

Momentum ring: encouragement, quantified

"3 of 5 sessions this week" turns practice into a gentle streak rather than a grade. It celebrates effort (sessions done) instead of only scores — kinder for a nervous 11-year-old.

Scores in the nav, colour-coded

Each topic shows its latest average in heatmap colours; untouched topics show a quiet "—" instead of an alarming zero. The collapsed Mathematics section still shows all 20 topics as a micro-heatmap strip.

Focus mode during timed practice

When the countdown starts, the rail collapses to a 44-px icon strip — navigation disappears, distraction disappears, and only a small amber timer chip remains.

Timed practice — calm, full-width, no nav
Section labels give the rail a spine

"Subjects" and "Coach" separate the student's world from the parent's. Admin lives under Coach rather than floating at the bottom behind an unexplained teal border.

Beyond the sidebar

Carry the amber "Up next" language everywhere a pending worksheet appears so students learn one signal; keep buttons solid brand colours (never gradients); and add a subtle press animation on Start — one moment of delight where it matters.
