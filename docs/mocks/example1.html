Why this works & further suggestions

One colour per subject, not blue everywhere

Today every icon, link, hover and active state is the same blue family, so nothing stands out. Here blue means Writing, green means Mathematics, amber means "action waiting for you" — the brand palette becomes wayfinding.

Mastery pips: the nav is also a progress map

Each topic link carries a small heatmap-coloured pip, and a collapsed section shows a micro-heatmap strip. A student sees where they're strong without opening the Dashboard — and red pips gently invite practice.

Search for 31 destinations

11 writing types + 20 math topics is too many for scanning. A "Jump to a topic" filter (⌘K) keeps the rail short — show 5–6 per section with "All 11 →" overflow links instead of one endless list.

"Up next" keeps pending worksheets one tap away

The footer card surfaces the oldest pending worksheet with its time budget. The student never hunts through sections; the parent sees at a glance that homework is set.

Solid pill for the active page

The current pale blue-on-blue active state is nearly invisible. A solid blue pill with white text is unambiguous, and the pip stays visible inside it.

Beyond the sidebar

Greet the student by name with one actionable insight ("Fractions is the one to watch"); use a consistent 8-px spacing rhythm and 40-px touch targets for young hands; and during Timed Practice, collapse the rail entirely so the countdown screen stays calm.
